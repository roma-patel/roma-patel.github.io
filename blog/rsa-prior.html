The Effect of Priors in Communication Games

The Rational Speech Acts (henceforth, RSA) model attempts to characterise pragmatic reasoning in situations that require speakers and listeners (i.e., agents in a cooperative communicative setting) to iteratively choose and interpret a referential signal.

http://www.markblokpoel.com/blog/2018/11/13/simulation-bias-why-analyzing-simulation-trials-is-imperative

There is unbounded work in old-school classical decision making, planning and also psychology in what is broadly termed the _anchoring and adjustment_ problem. In short, we see that bias increases with anchor extremity. Tie this is in with listener decisions, when they start with a certain prior - if you have a very low (or alternatively very high) prior over a referent to begin with, you are anchored to your decision, and adjustment from this point (e.g., moving towards the optimal direction) cannot happen - especially not until we have more world knowledge and more capacity (parameters?). Other work shows that ways to deal with the anchor and adjustment problem are to _increase_ knowledge capacity, to help abolish the bias, and therefore help decision making.
